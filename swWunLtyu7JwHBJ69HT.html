The psychology of wealth. What the rich know (and what the poor don't know)

In order to become rich, you do not need to thoroughly study the world of money and Finance. First of all, to do this, you should study how people behave with money.

And here you will find a lot of strange and even paradoxical!

It turns out that often we ourselves (with our own hands) "push" money and good opportunities to increase income from ourselves. And then we wonder why we are chronically lacking moneys'.

The psychology of wealth. What the rich know (and what the poor don't know):

1) the richest investor of our time

Warren Buffett (for many years one of the top three richest people on the planet) has built his own a multibillion-dollar fortune through investment.

More than 2 thousand books have been written, in which various authors try to understand exactly how Warren Buffett managed to get rich: 

  • But all their arguments can be summed up in one simple phrase: "This guy has been investing constantly all his life."
  • Exactly in the this is the secret of his wealth — he constantly invested money (making them work for him). 

2) Compound interest will make you rich

Making a good investment does not mean making the highest returns. On the contrary, the highest returns are the result of randomness (i.e., an accident). one-time, non-recurring payments).

In order to get rich, it is enough to get an average return on the market.

It will allow you to form an impressive capital (for many years of constant investment).

What to do: Make a good habit with every paycheck you make buy currencies, stocks, and other financial instruments.

3) wealth Is something you don't see.

If you spend the money you earn only on things, then you will eventually get things (but not wealth).

People who drive very expensive cars are usually not rich. After all they spent most of their money recklessly on buying a car (i.e., a nice piece of iron).

If you see someone driving a $ 200,000 car, the only thing you can say about that person is that they now have $ 200,000 less than they had before the purchase car's.

We tend to judge other people's wealth by what we see (and what they look like):

  • We can't see the size of their Bank account or their investment account statement.
  • Therefore, we rely entirely (and quite wrongly) on their appearance when estimating their size financial status (looking at cars, mansions, travel, etc. photo in Instagram).

The real wealth is invisible (because it lies in your Bank account and works around the clock, being properly invested in reliable investments).

4) Spend a million or earn?

This it would be cruel. But the truth of life is that you need to talk about it openly. 

When people say "I want to be a millionaire", most of them mean "I want to spend a million". 

And this, as you now understand, is the exact opposite of getting rich (and becoming a millionaire). millionaire).

SUMMARY:

Focus on using your regular investments to create assets that will pay you (bring in new money) again and again.
